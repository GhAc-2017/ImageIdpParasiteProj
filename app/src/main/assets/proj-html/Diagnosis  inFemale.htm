The Diagnosis of Trichomoniasis in the Female

Two methods are available for the diagnosis of trichomoniasis in the female. The parasites themselves often may be identified in vaginal discharge or specific agglutinins to T. foetus may be demonstrated in the vaginal mucus. Both of these tests gives viable results in an animal known to be infected and examinations for this disease in females must, therefore, be applied on a herd basis in the expectation that where the disease exist at least a few animals will give positive results.
