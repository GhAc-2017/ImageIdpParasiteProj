Examination of mucus for T.foetus

As with preputial washing, the parasites may be detected by direct microscopical and cultural examinations. In pyometra cases the flagellates are nearly always present. In other cases the parasites are present in vaginal discharges just after abortion, or in later stages of the disease, just prior to oestrus.

Because of the possible presence of antibodies in the mucus, it is important that it should be examined as soon as possible after collection.
