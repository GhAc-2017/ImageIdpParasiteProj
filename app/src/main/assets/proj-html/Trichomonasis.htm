Trichomonasis

The family Trichomonadidae includes 3 genera – Tritrichomonas with three anterior flagella, Trichomonas with four and Pentatrichomonas with five.

Most vertebrates harbour one or more species as normal commensals in the gut. Trichomonas vaginalis and Trichomonas gallinae are pathogenic species in humans and pigeons respectively.

Tritrichomonas foetus is a cause of infertility in cattle in which it also causes abortion, usually during the first 8 week of pregnancy.

The disease is venereal and the infection once setablished in the bull may persist for the animal’s lifetime. In the female however, the infection is usually self-limiting.
