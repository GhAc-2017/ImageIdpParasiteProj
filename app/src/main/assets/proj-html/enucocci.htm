Enumeration of coccidial oocysts

The enumeration of coccidial oocysts in samples of faeces and litter by the modified McMaster technique :-

Method :

     1.    Take 3g of faces or litter and mix throughly with 42ml* of tap water in a 120 ml wide                mouthed glass-stoppered bottle with glass balls.

    2.    Pour faecal suspension through wire mesh screen of aperture 0.15mm, collecting              filtrate in a clean, dry bowl.

    3.    Mix the filtrate throughly to ensure that there is a uniform suspension of farcal              material and transfer an aliquote to centrifuge tube filling to within 5mm of the top.              (If required the remainder of the fluid in the bowl may be mixed with an equal              volume of 4% potasium dicromate and put in a shallow dish at 27oC to allow the              oocysts to sporulated.

    4.    Centrifuge for 2 minutes at 800 g.

    5.    Discare the supernatant and emulsify the packed sediment.

    6.    Add saturated salt solution (NaCl), until the volume equals that of the initial aliquot of              filtrate. At this stage further dilution to 1/10 or 1/100 in saturated salt may be                 necessary if oocyst numbers are large.

    7.    Invert the tube several times until the sediment is evenly suspended and fill two              chambers of a McMaster slide using a clean Pasteur pipette. McMaster slides with six              vertical columns facilitate scanning under the microscope.

    8.    Count all the oocysts within the ruled area (1 cm2) of each chamber using the 10x              objective and 10x eyepiece, and calculate the mean value (x).

 

Calculation

Number of oocysts per g faeces= x * 100
Where
X Average number of oocysts in counting chamber
------- = -----------------------------------------------------------------

0.15 Volume of sample in 1 cm2

x 45 = Total volume of sample, i.e., 3 g feces +42 ml water.

X 1/3 = Correction to 1 g of faeces.
