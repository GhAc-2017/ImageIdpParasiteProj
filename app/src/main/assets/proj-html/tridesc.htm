TRICHOMONIASIS

The family Trichomonadidae includes 3 genera- Tritrichomonas with three anterior flegella, trichomonas with four and Pentatrichomonas with five.

most vertibrates harbour one or more species as normal commensals in the gut. Trichomonas vaginalis and Trichomonas gallunae are pathogenic species in humans and pigeons respectively.

Tritrichomonas foetus us a cause of infertility in cattle in which it also causes abrotion., usually during the first 8 weeks of pregnancy.

The disease is venereal and the infection once established in the bull may persist for the animal's ligetime. In the female however, the infection is usually self-limiting.
