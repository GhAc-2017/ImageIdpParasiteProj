Microscopical and cultural examinations

Preputial washing are spun in a centrifuge at 350 g for 10 minutes. The supernatant fluid is discarded. A drop of the centrifuged deposit is placed on a microscope slide and carefully examined after putting a coverslip. Any motile protozoa should be checked for the presence of anterior flagella and an undulating membrane. T

Movement may also be observed in the washing due to presemce of motile spermatozoa, motile bacteria, Brownian movement, or by free-living flagellates and ciliates. Some of the free living protozoa bear a close resemblance to T. foetus and it is important to be fully aware of the possibility of their occurrence in preputial washing from bulls.

About 0.1 to 0.2 ml of preputial washing deposit is inoculated into each culture tube.

Cultures are incubated at 370C for a minimum of 7 days. On the 4th day and finally on the 7th day a drop of the culture is removed aseptically and examined under the microscope for the presence of trichomonads.
