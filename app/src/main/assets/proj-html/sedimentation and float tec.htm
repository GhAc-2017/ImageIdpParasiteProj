Simple Floatation

1.     1 gm of the faeces is thoroughly mixed with 20 ml of floatation fluid.

2.    The suspension is passed through a sieve and the filtrate is caught in a test  tube.

3.    The fluid in the test  tube is allowed to stand for at least 30 minutes.

4.    A sample of the surface film removed with the wire loop or pipette and placed on a          microscope slide.

5.    A cover slip is placed on the glass slide.

6.    The preparation is examined using the low power (16 mm) objective of the            microscope.

Concentration by floatation using the Centrifuge

The Procedure involves the following :

1. 1 gm of faeces is mixed with about 30 ml of water.

2.    The mixture is then strained through a sieve.

3.    After straining, the filtrate is used to fill two centrifuge tubes.

4.    The mixture is centrifuged for 2 minutes at 1500 r.p.m.

5.    The supernatant fluid in the tubes is discarted and the tubes are refilled with clean         water, the process being repeated until the supernatant fluid is clear.

6.    The clear supernatant fluid is removed and the tubes are half filled with floatation         fluid.

Concentration by Sedimentation and Floatation

Procedure for concentration by sedimentation and floatation

First Stage - Screening and Sedimentation

1.    About 50 gm of faeces are placed in a glass bottle with about 50 ml of water and some          glass beads.

2.   The bottle is shaken vigorously.

3.    The mixture is passed through a wire sieve and filtrate is caught in a bowl; before         removal,  the sieve is rinsed with a little clean water.

4.    The contents of the bowl are poured into a measuring cylinder or similar tall                  container.

5.    The solution is then allowed to stand for at least three hours or overnight.

6.    The utensils, sieve, bowl and glass beads that have been used are thoroughly washed         and cleaned so that they are ready for re-use.

       

      Second stage - floatation and Sedimentation

1.    Within  3 hours the oocyst sedimentation has been completed and the oocysts are all         on the bottom of the container. The supernatant fluid is siphoned off so that the only          fluid  right at the bottom of the container remains. The supernatant fluid is discarded.

2.    The residual suspension is shaken and poured into centrifuge tubes, which are spun         for 2 minutes at about 1500 r.p.m.

3.    The supernatant fluid in the tubes is poured off and discarded.

4.    The tubes containing the residue are then filled with saturated salt solution.

5.    The tubes are spun in a centrifuge for 2 minute at 1500 r.p.m.

6.    The oocysts have now floated to the top of the floatation fluid, so the top quarter of         the salt solution in each tube is poured into a tall container of water.

7.    The oocysts are again allowed to settle (sedimentation) for at least 3 hours, or                overnight if more convenient.

       

      Third Stage - completion of Sedimentation and Examination

1.    After 3 hours, sedimentation being completed, the supernatant fluid is siphoned off         and discarded.

2.    The residual suspension is then poured into one or two centrifuge tubes and spun in         the centrifuge for 2 minutes at about 1500 revolution per minute.

3.    The supernatant fluid is poured off and discarded.

4.    Some of the sedimentation is removed with a wire loop, placed on a slide and covers         with a cover slip.

5.    The specimen is then examined under the low power (16 mm) objective of the                 microscope.

6.    The remaining residue in the tubes is shaken up with a 2 percent potassium                   dichromate solution, and can be kept in a petri dish, but the depth of the fluid must not exceed 1 centimeter to ensure satisfactory oxygen conditions for the development of oocysts.
