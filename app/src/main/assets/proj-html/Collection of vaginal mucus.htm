Collection of vaginal mucus from cattle

For the mucus agglutination test, undiluted mucus should be collected with an open-ended tube. If difficulty is encountered, and for cultural and microscopical examination, the following method may be used (Lander 1983).

Equipment required
    1. 20 ml lure fitting disposable syringe.
    2. Fairly rigid polythene tubing approx 0. 45 cm in length; 6mm external, and 4 mm internal diameter, closed at one end by gently heating in a flame.

      3. About 16 holes are made within 3 cm of the closed end using a hot needle. They are apread around the circumference in such a way that the collecting fluid can be sprayed in all directions.

    3. Universal containers of sterile phosphate buffered saline pH 7.2 (P.B.S)

Method
    1. Sterile PBS is drawn into the plastic tube fitted to the 20 ml disposable syringe so that air is excluded. The blocked end of the tube is inserted into the vagina as far as cervix.
    2. The saline is expelled forcefully into the vagina and sucked back into the syringe 4-6 times.
    3. It may be necessary to move the tube backwards along the floor of the vagina in order to locate the liquid. The contents are expelled back into the universal containers. A saperate tube and syringe are used for each animal. Tubes may be disinfected by boiling and reused.
