Leishman Stain

The stain is made by :

    1. Putting 0.15 gram Leishman powder into morter.
    2. Pouring in 100 ml pure methyl alcohol.
    3. Dissolving the powder by grinding in the morter.
    4. Then filtering the solution.

Leishman stain contains methyl alcohol; the stain is therefore used to fix microscope slide preparations. Preliminary fixation by alcohol is not needed.

The procedure for Leishman staining :

    1. Making a thin blood smear and allowing it to dry throughly.
    2. Applying 10 drops of the stain to the slide.
    3. Rocking the slide gently for about 30 seconds.
    4. Adding 20 drops of slightly alkaline distilled water.
    5. Mixing the stain and water by gently rocking the slide.
    6. Allowing 5 to 10 minutes for staining.
    7. Flooding the stain in a stream of distilled water until the water looks pink. (about 1 minutes)
    8. Leaving the slides in a vertical position so that it drains and dries.
    9. Examination without a cover slip.
