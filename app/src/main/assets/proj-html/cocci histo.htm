Schizogony and gametogony may be observed by examining fresh or giemsa stained smears of the infected tissue or by preparing stained sections.tissue may be fixed in 10% buffered formalin, but brilliant staining can be obtained after fixation in Zenker or Bouin’s solution, particularly with avian tissue. The most useful staining techniques are Ehrlich’s Haematoxylin and Eosin, and Regaud’s Iron Haematoxylin. For special purposes, Periodic acid-Schiff for Polysaccharide and Haematoxylin (PAS-H) is a useful method for the demonstration of gametocytes in sections of Zenker or formalin-fixed tissue. The AF-AO method consists of acid fuchsin staining followed by aniline blue-orange G. the technique is suitable for the demonstration of early schizogony stages in which the refractile globule of the sporozoite stains a brilliant red.
