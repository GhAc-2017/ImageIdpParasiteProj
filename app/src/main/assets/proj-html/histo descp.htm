HISTOMONIASIS

Formarely an importent disease of turkeys and chickens, histomoniasis (blackhead) has been controlled by environmental housing for poultry and effective prevention medication. the infection may persist in birda on free range and particularly in other gallinaceous species such as pheasants and peacocks. An enterohepatitis is caused by the protozoan histomonas maleagridis, which may be found in large numbers in the typical necrotic lesions in the caeca and liver.

Apert from recognition of the lesions, the most reliable method of diagnosis of blackhead is the identification of the parasites in stained sections of freahly fixed tissue. They usually do not stain with H & E but Short and Cooper's modification of the Giemsa staining technique for sections has been used successfully:

Method

        1. Fix in Carnoy's fluid; 60 ml ethyl wlcohol, 30 ml chloroform, 10 ml glacial               acetic acid, for 3-6 hours.

        2. Wash in 90% alcohol - 2 changes, 1 hour each.

        3. Dhydrate and embed in paraffin wax.

        4. Bring sections down to water.

        5. Stain 1 hour or longer in Giemsa stain 10 ml.

                                                        Aceton 10 ml

                                                        Pure methyl alcohol 10ml.

                                                        Distilled water (pH-7.2 phosphate) 100 ml.

        6. Wash rapidly in tap water and differentiate in 15% colophonium resin in              acetone.

        7. Wash in acetone: xylene 70:30. check under low power.

        8. clear in xylene, mount in Green Euparal.
