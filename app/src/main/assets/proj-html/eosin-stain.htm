The use of Eosin stain

Eosin stain (0.5 percent aqueous solution of eosin) is sometimes used during the microscopic examination of fresh faeces since this stain colours the faecal debris into intense red; but freshly passed protozoa, including cysts, resist the stain. They are easily identified under the low power (16 mm) objective when they are seen as "pearly" white bodies against a pink background.

Sometimes a direct smear is made of faeces, the following methods usually being employed.

    1. A few drops of eosin stain are placed on a clean slide.
    2. A small amount of faeces is emulsified on the slide with the eosin stain.
    3. All large particles of faecal material are removed.
    4. A cover slip is placed on the smear.
    5. The preparation is examined under a microscope using the low power (16mm) objective.
