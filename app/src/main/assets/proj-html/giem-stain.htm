Giemsa Staining

The normal procedure for Giemsa staining :

    1. Making a smear and allowing it to dry throughly.
    2. Fixing in methyl alcohol for 2 minutes.
    3. Mixing 1 ml of Giemsa stain in a petri dish with 9 ml of slightly alkaline distilled water (pH 7.0 to 7.2).
    4. Leaving the slide inverted on two half slides in the petri dish to stain for 15 to 30 minutes.
    5. Flooding the slide with distilled water until the smear is just pink, usually about 1 minute.
    6. Allow to dry.
    7. Examining the slide when dry under the microscope without a cover slip.

The procedure for Prolonged Giemsa staining:

    1. A smear is made and allowed to dry.
    2. Fix in methyl alcohol for 2 minutes.
    3. The stain and slightly alkaline distilled water are put into a petri dish; the ratio of stain to water is 1 ml of stain to 50 ml of water.
    4. The slide is inverted and placed on two half slides in the petri dish so that the material on the slide makes contact with the stain.
    5. It is then left for 16 to 24 hours.
    6. The slide is washed in distilled water until the smear is just pink, usually about one minute.
    7. The slide is allowed to dry.
    8. The slide is examined under the microscope without a cover slip.
