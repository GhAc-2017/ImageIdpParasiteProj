Morphologicla Examination of Oocysts

 

A clean preparation of oocysts for morphological examination can be obtained using a modification of salt flotation technique.

Method

  1. Emulsification faeces are squeezed through muslin to remove coarse particles and sedimentated in a centrifuge tube with ground –glass top at 800 g for 2 minutes.
  2. The sedimentis emulsified and the tube is filled with saturated salt solution, inverted several times and placed in a centrifuge. Salt solution is then added until the meniscue appears convex.
  3. Place an 18x 18mm coverglass on top of the tube and centrifuge for 2 minutes at 200g.
  4. Remove the coverglass carefully with a deliberate upward movement.place the coverglass on a clean slide and examine at least 100 oocysts.

Oocysts from cattle,, sheep, rabbits, etc., can be differentiated more readily into species after sporulation. A simple culture may be prepared by emulsifying a sample of faeces with 2% potassium dichromate and straining it through muslin to form a shallow layer in a petri dish. The oocysts are allowed to sporulated at room temperature and are examined at intervals until sporulation is complete in 3-4 days.

Many details of oocyst structure can be seen only under an oil immersion objective. Coverslip preparation used for this purpose should be sealed with petrolium jelly.
