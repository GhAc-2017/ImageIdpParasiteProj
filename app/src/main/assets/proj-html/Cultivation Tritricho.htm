Cultivation of Tritrichomonas foetus

Many different media have been used for the cultivation of T. foetus. Following is a widely used media for culture and isolation of strains from the field.

Glucose-borth-serum medium (G.B.S.)

Method :
    1. Inspissated horse serumslopes are prepared in test-tubes by heating 4-5 ml amounts of normal horse serum in a sloping position at 80 0C for 2 hours.
    2. Glucose-peptone broth (Clausen’s modification) is prepared by dissolving the following in warm distilled water:

      2.0% Neopeptone (Difco)

      1.0% Lemco (Oxoid)

      0.5% Proteolysed liver extract (Panmede)

      2.0% Glucose

      Adjusted pH 7.4 and sterilised by Seitz filtration (EKS pad).

    • The sterile broth is added to the test tubes to cover the serum slopes and sterile liquid paraffin is finally added to form a thin surface layer.

Growth in this medium with an inoculum of about 0.5 ml should be visible after 24 hours. Subinoculations should be carried out every other day, with incubation at 37 0C. for the isolation of parasites from pathological material, a mixure of penicillin and streptomycin, 100-500 units per ml, should be added to the medium before inoculation. Addition of both these antibiotics usually give complete control of bacterial growth. Fungi and resistant strains of bacteria may be controlled by the further addition of polymyxin.
