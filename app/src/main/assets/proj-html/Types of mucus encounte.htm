Types of mucus encountered

a). Vaginal mucus

Thick, scanty, uniformly turbid. Frequently rich in agglutinins if the cow is infected. Rarely contains motile trichomonads. Present in the vagina between oesrtrous period, epecially during the first half of the oestrus cycle.

b). Uterine mucus

Thinner, clear. May contain flakes of pus and motile trichomonads if the cow is infected. Rarely contains agglutinins. May be present in the vagina especially during the week preceeding oestrus, when the cervix is beginning to relex.

c). Oestral mucus

Clear and copious. Dose not usually contain either agglutinins or trichomonads.

d). Post oestral mucus

For 2 or 3 days after oestrus, vaginal mucus may contain blood. This makes the interpretation of the test more difficult, as blood from uninfected cattle normally contains agglutinins which can give a false positive reaction.

 

For the agglutination test

                    The best time to take samples is 5-10 days after oestrus.

For direct examination

                    The best time to take samples is during the week before oestrus.
