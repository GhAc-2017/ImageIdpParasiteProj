Staining of Blood Slide

The following methods are used for fixing material on slides for preservation and/or staining.

Absolute Alcohol :-

This is suitable for blood films and smears.

    1. Slides are immersed in the alcohol for 15 minutes.
    2. If an aqueous stain is to be used, the slides must be dipped in water after fixation.

Methyl alcohol (acetone free)

This is suitable for blood films and smears.

    1. Slides are immersed for 2 minutes.
    2. If an aqueous stain is to be used, the slides must be dipped in water after fixation.

Corrosive Sublimate :-

This is a saturated solution of mercuric chloride in physiological saline.

    1. Slides are immersed in the solution for one to two minutes.
    2. Slides are washed well in water before staining.
