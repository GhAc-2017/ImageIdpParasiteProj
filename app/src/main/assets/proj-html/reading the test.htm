Reading the test

The agglutination results produced by different concentrations of antibodies are recorded as described below. :-

+++++ Very large clumps with little or no mortility.

++++ Large clumps, very compact, with the flagella of the organisms slightly active.

+++ Larger clumps of active organisms with a few free unagglutinated organisms.

++ Small clumps of agglutinated organisms with high motility.

+ a dispersed culture with occasional small aggregations of a few active organisms, many unagglutinated organisms.

    The rationale of these values may be described as follows :

                        The motility of the trichomonad is well maintained in the lower cocentrations of antibodies and the organisms may contact one another frequently, become loosely attached and easily break free again. The normal morphology is preserved as the agglutinin concentration increases, the protozoa become more firmly adherent. On contact, a few break free larger clumps are formed, in which the motility is sufficient to rotate the agglutinated masses forming them into rounded clumps.

A+++ result given by a 1/20 dilution of mucus in this test is indicative of the presence of infection. It should always be confirmed by demonstrating the presence of the parasite.
