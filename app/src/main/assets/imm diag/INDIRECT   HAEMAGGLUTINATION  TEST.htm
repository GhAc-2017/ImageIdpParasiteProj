INDIRECT HAEMAGGLUTINATION TEST

(Using SHEEP RBC)

The agglutination of a particulate antigen by its specific antibody is, classically, the sinplest way of estimating the quantity of antibody in a serum. Red blood cells have been found to be extremly convenient passive carriers of antigen. Coated cells become most sensitive and stable reagent for antibody detection. Some antigens particularly the polysaccharides can be effectively coated by only exposing red cells for a short time. Among the general methods available for coating antigen on red blood cells the most widely used method is the tanned cell technique. Treatment of the red blood cells with tannic acid increases the amount of most protein antigens subsequently adsorbed. The higher density of coated antigen greatly increases the sensitivity of the agglutination reaction. The test can also be used in a reverse manner where tanned red blood cells can also be coated with antibody  test the presence of antigen.

 

 

Reagents and materials :

  • Alsever's solution : Dextrose 2.05 gms; sodium citrate 0.80 gms; sodium chloride 0.42 gms, dissolve in 90 ml distilled water. Adjust pH to 6.1 with 10% citric acid make up the volume to 100 ml and autoclave the solution. Phosphate buffered saline (pH 7.2) Stock solution (PBS): Na2H Po4 (anhydrous) 36.0 g; KH2PO4 (anhydrous) 2.15 g ; NaCl 36.0 g; dissolve in distilled water and make upto 1 litre. Store the buffer at 4 degree Centrigrade. Stock buffer can be diluted 1:5 just before use.

  • Antigen solution : 

  • Tannic acid solution (5 mg in 50 ml PBS) : A good reagent grade tannic acid should be used. It is preferable to make up the solution freshly each time.

  • Sheep red blood cells (SRBC) : Sheep blood stored at 4 degree C after mixing with an equal volume of Alsevers' solution.

  • Normal rabbit serum (used as stabilizer) : Inactivated serum by heating at 56 degree C for 20 minutes ( to destroy complements) and absorbed twice with a quarter of its volume of packed SRBC from the same batch as those being coated.
