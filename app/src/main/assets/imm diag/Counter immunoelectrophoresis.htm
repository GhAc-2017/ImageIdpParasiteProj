Counter immunoelectrophoresis

Reagents Test Result Animation

Counterimmunoelectrophoresis, also known as crossover electrophoresis or immunoelectrosonophoresis, makes use of endosomtic flow, which during electrophoresis causes the slow moving gammaglobulins to be displaced towards the cathode. In this manner the antibodies move as a concentrated front toward the cathode, while the antigens move in the opposite direction toward the anode. By appropriate arrangement of wells, antibody and antigen can thus be made to converge, resulting in the formation of a precipitate between two wells. The precipitin lines develop rapidly and usually reach maximum intensity in this system within 30 to 90 minutes, depending on the strength of the reagents.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Reagents

  • 0.05 M barbitine buffer (pH 8.0)

  • 1% Difco bacto agar in Barbitone buffer

  • Electrophoresis apparatus

  • Glass slides

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Test procedure

  • Pour 10 ml of hot 1% agar on a glass plate. Allow it to solidify and keep at 40 C in a humid chamber for 30 minutes.

  • Cut two parallel rows of wells with the help of a template. Each well is 3 mm in diameter with 5 mm between antigen and antibody wells.

  • Place the plate in electrophoresis chamber, so that antibody wells are always oriented on the anodal side while the antigen well is towards cathode.

  • Fill the row of wells with antibody (towards positive elctrode) and antigen (towards negative electrode)

  • Place the slides in chamber, place paper wicks and starts electrophoresis at 200 volts for 90 to 120 minutes at room temperature.

  • After elcetrophoresis, the plate was washed with several changes in normal saline solution for 24 hours to remove the free protein from the gel.

  • Subsequent washing was carried out by distilled water to remove the salts.

  • The palte was covered with good quality lint free filter paper and dried overnight.

  • The filter papers were removed from the slides and the plate was stained with 0.1% Commassie brilliant blue R-250 (0.1% commassie blue, 41.7% methanol, 16.7% acetic acid) for 30 minutes and then destained using destaning solution (30% meyhanol, 10% acetic acid0 till the background become clear.
